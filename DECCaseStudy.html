NYS Department of Environmental Conservation -Redesigned-

Responsive Web Design
GOAL
To make the DEC Website more user friendly and appeal to a more general audience
MY ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES

UX Researcher Information Architect UI Designer

Tools

Figma Illustrator Adobe XD

Timeline
4 weeks

There was a big learning curve to this project as we did not have access to stakeholders. This, paired with the very difficult navigation and organization of the site, made it very difficult to decipher what the goal/purpose of the site was. I started by creating a proto-persona and user scenario in hopes that this would help me better understand the intended user and what they might go to this site for. Initially because of the large “Love My Park Day” banner displayed on the homepage, I thought the site would be focused on volunteer opportunities and activism. However, through much investigation I discovered it was really geared towards education and information on regulations and policies.

Though I think there really could be two separate user groups - everyday citizens and policy makers - I wanted to focus on making the site more accessible to everyday people. Even without speaking to stakeholders I could see efforts to do this already on the site by including the Twitter feed as well as some of the family-oriented imagery, but found the content to be very difficult to understand, leaving myself and other potential users extremely frustrated. Being that the site was so vast in information and possible user flows, I decided for this project to focus on revamping the homepage and main navigation as well as a path I thought my proto-persona would typically take.

Proto Persona

User Scenario

Sean is visiting the DEC website because he loves doing outdoor activities such as hiking, hunting and fishing but he knows that these things impact the environment. Sean is worried about the future of the planet for his kids and would like to do his part in protecting its resources. Sean wants to be more informed on the hunting and fishing regulations so he can continue to do what he loves more responsibly.

I wanted to test the initial site in order to see the pain points a user might have when trying to work through a typical flow. As suspected, there were many challenges, especially with the navigation. Each of my users found different ways to to complete the task and only one of them used what I had determined was the most direct path. Many wanted to go straight to the search function as they found the site was much too overwhelming. Overall the testers found that the site was very dense and text heavy. This was very much in line with what I found when completing my heuristic evaluation. The combined knowledge from user tests and heuristic evaluation validated that my focus/priority needed to be on the main navigation.

Goals/Objectives:

  • To Find the pain points in using the current website
  • To gain better understanding of what the user gravitates towards on the site

Task:

  • “You are going on a fishing trip and need to understand the regulations mandated by NYS”

Steps:

  1. Go to DEC Website
  2. Find fishing page
  3. Locate fishing regulations
  4. Assess Statewide regulations
  5. Assess regulations by region

Sucsess Criteria

  • User has found and understands regulations and feels confident they have all the info they need.

Average SUS Score: 51.5

Task Completion Rate:80%

Takeaways:

  • Site is very “dense”. There is way too much text.
  • Headers are unclear
  • Difficult to navigate and locate where you are on the site
  • Seems easy enough on a high level but when you get into the more specific details it is confusing
  • Map and graphics need to be updated
  • Not good recovery for error
